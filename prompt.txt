TutorBot — Capability Film · build spec of record
(reflects the delivered film: film_MKfeedback1/ → TutorBot_Film_MKfeedback1.html)

This is a browser-based demo video for TutorBot (by bodhan.ai): an animated, timed,
autoplaying HTML/CSS/JS experience that plays like a short explainer film, not a static page.
On-screen text + product UI carry the story; a music bed plays underneath, and two short TTS
voice clips play on the teacher-tool beats (see below). It runs as ONE continuous film of
20 beats: 15 content scenes + 5 quiet "Interlude" chapter cards between the major sections.

STEP 0 — ORIENT YOURSELF FIRST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
1. Engine: forked from the AI4B-Transcribe capability-film engine (a `Reel` sequencer that
   walks scenes; each scene runs a keyframe timeline for video-style staged reveals). Visual
   language — warm orange/peach product-native look, rounded cards.
2. Type system: UI face is **Inter** (weights 300–800, bundled offline). Indian scripts render
   in **Noto Sans <script>** (Devanagari, Telugu, …). A 4-tier type scale drives everything —
   display / headline / body / label — with matching weights (mirrors the team design tokens).
3. Real assets live in `Resources/` (capital R):
   - `Bodhanlogo.png` — official Bodhan origami-swan mark. Keyed to transparent; saved as
     `images/bodhan_mark.png`. Used at the gate, fixed brand mark, chat avatars, and end card.
     (On the dark end card the mark is tinted white to stay visible.)
   - `child_safety.png` and other product captures (`LessonPlan1.png`, `hint.png`,
     `interactive_convexlens.png`, …) — inspiration for LIVE mocks; do NOT just show a screenshot.
   - `film/assets/india.svg` — per-state paths (data-state) for the closing map.
4. Audio:
   - Music bed → `audio/music.mp3` ("Saffron Ledger"). The earlier Sitar bed is kept as
     `audio/music_Sitar.mp3` if a swap is ever wanted. Music ducks under the interactive beats.
   - TTS voice clips (inlined into the portable build):
       `audio/tts_ashutosh.mp3` (Ashutosh) — plays on the Lesson-plans beat.
       `audio/tts_priya.mp3`   (Priya)   — plays on the Admin beat; the transcript typing is
       paced to this ~14.6 s clip. Music fades to ~6 % while a voice clip plays, then returns.
5. For beats with no matching screenshot (chats, pipeline, prompt→output, voice admin, the lens
   sim, the map) build lightweight LIVE mocked-up UI in HTML/CSS/SVG that matches the product look.

STEP 1 — BUILD REQUIREMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- Single-page app, vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, no build step. Editable copy/timing lives in `reel.js`;
  scene builders + sequencer in `app.js`; per-scene themes in `styles.css`. `bundle.py` inlines
  every asset (music, TTS clips, fonts, images, india.svg) into one portable
  `../TutorBot_Film_MKfeedback1.html` that opens by double-click, no server.
- Autoplay, timed like a video. Controls: click / Space = pause · ← → = skip scene · Replay
  button on the end card. A chapter readout ("n / 20 · <name>") sits bottom-right; the Interlude
  cards read "n / 20 · Interlude".
- FIXED 16:9 CANVAS (locked layout): the whole film lives inside a `#screen` container that is
  always 16:9, sized to fit the viewport and centred (letterbox/pillarbox bars on off-16:9
  displays). ALL internal sizing uses container-query units (cqw/cqh) that resolve against this
  canvas — so every element holds its exact relative position and size on any browser or display.
  The layout must NOT reflow with window aspect ratio.
- Per-slide reveal pattern (every content slide follows this):
  1. the short LABEL (kicker: section/principle name) lands first;
  2. the HEADLINE streams in WORD-BY-WORD (token-style, with a caret) — never letter-by-letter;
  3. a brief pause;
  4. the MAIN CONTENT builds/fades in beneath the settled headline; any secondary label (e.g. the
     Scaffolding problem "Algebra · solve 2x + 3 = 11") appears only AFTER the headline finishes.
  Letter-by-letter TYPING is reserved for moments where something is literally being entered/
  generated: the teacher prompt boxes and the admin voice transcript.
- Interludes: 5 standalone quiet chapter cards, one italic line each, on a calm backdrop, one
  before each major section (see scene list). They keep the narration lively between sections.
- Transitions between scenes: a soft cream crossfade (no hard wipes, no orange curtain).
- Do NOT use the term "system prompt" anywhere. Refer to it as the model's behaviour / how the
  model responds by default ("Built into TutorBot's Behaviour", "Pedagogy encoded into the model").
- Do NOT show "Section 1/2/3/4" (or B1/B2/B3) enumerations on screen — labels are plain names only.
- Do NOT label the chapter cards "Sutradhara" on screen — they read "Interlude".
- The Admin voice input is a mocked animation (waveform + scripted transcript typing on) paced to
  the Priya TTS clip — no microphone, no Web Speech API.
- Chat exchanges type on turn by turn (typing indicator → bubble), never all at once.

STEP 2 — CONTENT (20 beats, one continuous film)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1 — OPEN (title lockup)
"Evidence-based." → "Built for the" → "Indian classroom." → "Meet" → the TutorBot logo lockup
(official mark + wordmark) → specs beneath: "Grounded in NCF and learning science — an AI tutor,
and an AI for the teacher too." Warm, mote-flecked backdrop.

2 — THE FOUNDATION
Headline: "We did not start by building an AI chatbot. We started by studying how children learn."
A left-to-right pipeline builds ONE STAGE AT A TIME (stage → connector → next), as tall cards:
  NEP + NCF + Learning Science → Pedagogical Design → Built into TutorBot's Behaviour → AI Tutor.
(The last stage gets a highlighted "live" state.)

3 — INTERLUDE  ·  "That's the foundation. Now let's see a few principles in action."

4 — THE TUTORBOT PRINCIPLES (section intro)
Kicker: "The TutorBot Principles" (large). Headline: "Every interaction follows evidence-based
learning principles." Four dots hint at the four principles that follow.

5 — PRINCIPLE 1 · CONSTRUCTIVISM
Headline: "Children learn better by actively participating."
Chat: Student: "Why do we jerk forward when the bus suddenly stops?" · Tutor asks back: "Before I
explain — what was your body trying to do the moment before it stopped?"
Caption: "The tutor first understands the learner before teaching."

6 — PRINCIPLE 2 · DIALOGIC LEARNING
Headline: "Learning happens through conversation."
Chat: Student states a fact about photosynthesis · Tutor: "Can you explain this back to me in
your own words?" · Student explains it back (win).
Caption: "It doesn't check whether the student memorised the answer — it checks whether they
understood it."

7 — PRINCIPLE 3 · SCAFFOLDING
Headline: "Complex problems are broken into manageable steps."
The problem label "Algebra · solve 2x + 3 = 11" appears AFTER the headline, above the boxes.
Visual: the SAME guiding steps shown side by side in THREE language boxes — English · Telugu ·
Hindi — the Telugu and Hindi lines in NATIVE SCRIPT (not romanised), revealed one step at a time
across all three:
  EN: "Subtract 3 from both sides, what's left?" / "Good. Now divide both sides by 2." / "Excellent. So what is x?"
  TE (Telugu script) and HI (Devanagari) mirror the same three steps.
Caption: "Instead of giving away the solution, the tutor guides the student one step at a time —
in the language of the classroom."

8 — PRINCIPLE 4 · ACTIVITY-BASED LEARNING
Headline: "Understanding comes from exploration and interaction."
Visual: a BUILT interactive convex-lens ray simulation (SVG) — object arrow, bi-convex lens, F/2F
markers, three coloured rays converging to a real inverted image; a live stats panel shows object
distance (u), image distance (v), magnification (m) and image nature. Built live, not a screenshot.

9 — SAFE BY DESIGN
Headline: "Before it teaches anything, it keeps a child safe."
A live refusal chat types on (student asks how to fake a stomach ache to skip school; TutorBot
refuses, responds with care, points to a trusted adult) + a guardrail flow:
  🛑 Refuses the unsafe request → 💛 Responds with care, not alarm → 🧑‍🏫 Points to a trusted adult.

10 — INTERLUDE  ·  "Mapped these learning principles into one tutor."

11 — WHERE WE ARE TODAY
Headline: "The pedagogical groundwork is complete." Checklist, ticking in one item at a time:
  ✓ Learning principles mapped  ·  ✓ Pedagogy encoded into the model  ·
  ✓ Tutor behaviour specified   ·  ✓ Ready for classroom pilots

12 — INTERLUDE  ·  "Theory's settled, TutorBot's ready. Now the real test begins."

13 — CLASSROOM PILOTS ("The next nine months")
Headline: "Classroom pilots: Validate TutorBot across real classrooms."
Lead "What the pilots measure" + four tiles: Learning outcomes · Student engagement ·
Teacher adoption · Classroom usability.

14 — INTERLUDE  ·  "The classroom has two sides. This is the other one."

15 — THE TURN (Teachers need AI too)
Warm Indian-classroom backdrop (green chalkboard "½ + ¼ = ?", wall frame, barred window, fan,
bench-desks). Headline: "That was the student's side. Teachers need AI too."
Two panels: Student mode ✓ done (Socratic tutoring · Scaffolded hints · Activity-based learning)
→ Teacher mode · next (Differentiated worksheets · Dynamic lesson plans · Parent communication).

16 — DIFFERENTIATED WORKSHEETS
Teacher prompt types in: "Rahul struggles with fractions. Meena is good at fractions but weak in
word problems. Generate tomorrow's worksheet." Output: two worksheet cards — Rahul (fraction
basics, visual/single-step) and Meena (word problems, applied/multi-step).
Caption: "The system automatically produces differentiated worksheets tailored to each student."

17 — DIFFERENTIATED LESSON PLANS  (voiced: Ashutosh TTS clip; music ducks)
Teacher prompt (typed, in Hindi/Devanagari): "हमरे स्टूडेंट दरभंगा से हैं। आधी क्लास को फ्रैक्शन्स समझ में आ
गया है, बाकी बच्चा लोग अभी भी स्ट्रगल कर रहे हैं। कल के लिए एक अच्छा लेसन प्लान इंग्लिश में बनाइए।"
Output: a two-track plan (Still struggling / Ready to stretch) assembles side by side; then a live
self-localisation lands — the generic example is struck through and replaced, highlighted:
"Sharing a ~~pizza~~ roti equally between 4 friends — each hissa (share) is one tukda (piece),
or 1/4 of the whole." (Animated strikethrough-then-replace.)
Caption: "AI generates a lesson plan for mixed-ability classrooms — grounded in Darbhanga, not a
generic textbook default."

18 — ADMINISTRATIVE ASSISTANT (voice input; voiced: Priya TTS clip; music ducks)
Opens on a mic + waveform. The teacher "speaks" and a transcript types on, PACED to the Priya
voice clip: "Rahul had difficulty with reading today." / "Priya improved in multiplication." /
"Arjun was absent." / "Can you write a note for each parent. Rahul's mom prefers Marathi, and
Priya's family speaks Telugu at home." Then it hands off ("Generating parent notes…") and THREE
separate, personalised notes appear (not one generic summary):
  - Rahul's parents (Marathi–English): "Rahul la aaj reading jara tough vatla, pan amhi tyavar
    ekatra kaam karat aahot — kahi tension nahi, ya week madhey thoda extra practice karu."
  - Priya's parents (Telugu–English): "Priya multiplication lo chala baga improve ayyindi ee week
    — chala proud moment class ki!"
  - Arjun's parents (English): a warm, neutral note about the absence inviting a check-in.
Caption: "Three separate, personalised notes — not one generic summary. Written in each family's
own mix of languages: Marathi–English for Rahul, Telugu–English for Priya."

19 — INTERLUDE  ·  "That's what it does today. Here's where it's going."

  Phase A — kicker "The promise by December 2027" (large) + line "1M Teachers, AI-literate."
  Phase B — the India map (SVG) fills in, states light up across the country while a counter
    climbs to 1,000,000: "One million teachers, in every corner of India." · counter label
    "teachers, AI-literate" · "N / 36 states & union territories".
  Phase C — a high-contrast dark END CARD: the Bodhan mark (white on dark), eyebrow
    "bodhan.ai · TutorBot", title "Built for India, in India.", sub "Grounded in Learning
    Sciences & NCF 2023", line "AI that lifts both students and teachers." + a Replay button.
