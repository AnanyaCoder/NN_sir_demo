5 · PRINCIPLE 1 — CONSTRUCTIVISM
"Children learn better by actively participating."
  Student: "Why do we jerk forward when the bus suddenly stops?"
  TutorBot: "Before I explain — what was your body trying to do the moment before it stopped?"
The tutor first understands the learner before teaching.

6 · PRINCIPLE 2 — DIALOGIC LEARNING
"Learning happens through conversation."
  Student: "So plants make their food using sunlight."
  TutorBot: "Can you explain this back to me in your own words?"
  Student: "Leaves take in sunlight and… turn it into food for the plant?"
It doesn't check whether the student memorised the answer — it checks whether they understood it.

7 · PRINCIPLE 3 — SCAFFOLDING
"Complex problems are broken into manageable steps."
Problem: Algebra · solve 2x + 3 = 11   (appears after the headline)
Same three guiding steps, side by side in English · Telugu · Hindi (Telugu & Hindi in native script):
  EN: "Subtract 3 from both sides, what's left?" → "Good. Now divide both sides by 2." → "Excellent. So what is x?"
  TE: "రెండు వైపు నుంచి 3 తీసేయ్, ఏం మిగిలింది?" → "ఓకే! ఇప్పుడు రెండు వైపు 2 తో డివైడ్ చెయ్యి." → "సరే. ఇప్పుడు x ఎంత?"
  HI: "दोनों तरफ से 3 घटाओ, अब क्या बचा?" → "सही! अब दोनों को 2 से डिवाइड करो." → "बहुत बढ़िया. तो x क्या है?"
Instead of giving away the solution, the tutor guides the student one step at a time — in the
language of the classroom.

8 · PRINCIPLE 4 — ACTIVITY-BASED LEARNING
"Understanding comes from exploration and interaction."
VISUAL — A live, built convex-lens ray simulation (object arrow, bi-convex lens, F/2F markers,
three converging rays → real inverted image) with a live stats panel: object distance (u), image
distance (v), magnification (m), image nature. Past 2F the image is real/inverted/smaller;
between F and 2F it magnifies.

9 · SAFE BY DESIGN
"Before it teaches anything, it keeps a child safe."   (A real refusal)
  Student: "Sir, how can I fake a stomach ache? I don't want to go to school tomorrow."
  TutorBot: "I can't help with faking an illness — that could be unsafe."
  TutorBot: "It sounds like a really hard day. Please talk to a parent or a teacher you trust —
             they're there to help you. 💛"
🛑 Refuses the unsafe request → 💛 Responds with care, not alarm → 🧑‍🏫 Points to a trusted adult.

16 · DIFFERENTIATED WORKSHEETS
Teacher prompt: "Rahul struggles with fractions. Meena is good at fractions but weak in word
problems. Generate tomorrow's worksheet."
  Rahul — Fraction basics (visual, single-step): "Shade 1/2 of the circle." · "Which is bigger:
    1/4 or 1/2?" · "Write 3 out of 4 as a fraction."
  Meena — Word problems (applied, multi-step): "A recipe for 4 needs 3/4 cup of flour. Scale it
    for 6." · "2/3 of a 30-student class went on the trip. How many stayed?"
The system automatically produces differentiated worksheets tailored to each student.
